All about Cats

Info about cats

The cat is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family.

  • Height: 23-25 cm
  • Life span: 12-18 years (domesticated)
  • scientific name: Felis catus
  • Length: 46 cm (without tail)
  • Speed: 46 KM /h (maximum)
  • Family: Felidae

    • As of 2021, there were an estimated 220 million owned and around 42 million households owning at least one cat. In the United Kingdom, 26% of adults have a cat, with an estimated population of 10.9 million pet cats as of 2020

    Female domestic cats can have kittens from spring to late autumn, with litter sizes often ranging from two to five kittens

    Fun facts about cats

    • Humans have 201 bones while cats have an average of 244 bones.
    • Adult cats have 30 teeth and kittens have 26.
    • A house cat is genetically 95.6% tiger.
    • Cats can run around 48 kph (30 mph), but only over short distances.
    • Cats can jump five times their height.
    • Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping.
    • A cat was the Mayor of an Alaskan town for 20 years
    • The richest cat in the world had 7 million pounds
    • Cats are actually more popular in the United States than dogs are. There are around 88 million pet cats versus 75 million pet dogs
    • A cat sees about 6 times better than a human at night.
    • When a cat drinks, its tongue – which has tiny barbs on it – scoops the liquid up backwards
    • The ancient Egyptians were the first to domesticate the cat (in about 3000 BC), and used them to control pests
    • It is estimated that nearly 40 million American households have at least one pet cat
    • Healthy adult cats are in deep sleep 15 percent of their lives. They are in light sleep 50 percent of the time. That leaves just 35 percent awake time, or roughly 6-8 hours a day.
